ASHRAE Design Competition: São Paulo Public Library

Overview

Comprehensive HVAC system design for a three-story public library in São Paulo, Brazil (Climate Zone 2A). Designed innovative hybrid system integrating VRF-DOAS with renewable energy to achieve significant energy savings while maintaining optimal conditions for book preservation and occupant comfort.

Performance Achievements

  • 45% energy reduction compared to baseline system (284 to 157 kWh/m²)
  • 397,800 kWh/year solar energy generation from 250 kW roof PV system (232 panels)
  • Operational carbon reduced to 101 kgCO₂/m² (from 184 kgCO₂/m² baseline)
  • Energy cost reduced to $29/m² annually

System Design

  • Primary System: VRF (Variable Refrigerant Flow) with DOAS (Dedicated Outdoor Air System)
  • Dehumidification: Desiccant dehumidifiers for humid climate control
  • Ventilation: Energy Recovery Ventilators (ERV) for optimal indoor air quality
  • Renewable Energy: 250 kW rooftop photovoltaic system
  • Distribution: Overhead diffusers and linear grilles for targeted airflow

Facade & Envelope Optimization

  • Low-SHGC glass (U-value: 1.22 W/m²·K, SHGC: 0.28) for solar heat gain control
  • Double-skin facade systems with ventilated buffer zones
  • Dynamic shading devices (louvers and brise-soleil)
  • Green roof for heat island effect reduction

Zoning Strategy

  • Tailored thermal zoning for diverse library spaces (reading, storage, archives, offices)
  • Occupancy-based controls with sensors for energy optimization
  • 24/7 climate control for special collections (60°F DB, 30% RH minimum)
  • Flexible system supporting future space modifications

Tools & Standards

Climate Studio, Energy Modeling, ASHRAE Standards 62.1, 90.1, 55, Load Calculations, PVWatts Calculator

Small Office HVAC System Selection & Energy Analysis

Overview

Comprehensive HVAC system analysis for a small coworking office in San Francisco, CA. Evaluated multiple system types and recommended ductless mini-split VRF system achieving significant energy savings while meeting occupant comfort requirements.

Key Results

  • 33% energy savings with VRF system implementation
  • Site EUI reduced to 54 kBTU/ft² (from 81 kBTU/ft² baseline)
  • Peak cooling load: 79.7 kBTU/hr
  • Peak heating load: 61.3 kBTU/hr
  • Total fresh air requirement: 770.52 CFM

System Comparison & Selection

Evaluated four HVAC system types for small office application:

  • Ductless Mini-Split VRF (Selected): High efficiency, flexible zoning, minimal space requirements
  • VAV System: Good efficiency but higher installation costs and space requirements
  • Geothermal Heat Pump: Highest efficiency but prohibitive initial cost
  • WSHP: Efficient but requires significant mechanical space

Design Calculations

  • Zone-by-zone thermal load analysis (5 thermal zones)
  • Fresh air requirements per ASHRAE 62.1
  • Peak outdoor air heating load: 10,842 BTU/hr
  • Peak outdoor air cooling load: 5,073 BTU/hr
  • ERV sizing using RenewAire CORES calculator (HE 10IN unit selected)

Selected Equipment

  • VRF System: Mitsubishi Electric PUMY-P60 (20,000 BTU/h cooling, 24,000 BTU/h heating)
  • Performance: SEER 18, HSPF 8.2
  • Configuration: Supports 5 indoor units for multi-zone control
  • ERV: RenewAire HE 10IN (250-1100 CFM capacity)

Additional Strategies

  • External shading devices for solar heat gain control (increased savings to 66%)
  • Building envelope improvements reducing infiltration
  • LED lighting upgrades for 73% lighting energy reduction
  • Smart controls and occupancy sensors

Tools & Standards

Climate Studio, ASHRAE Standards 62.1 & 90.1, Load Calculations, RenewAire CORES, Energy Modeling

Adaptive Reuse: Office-to-Residential Conversions

Overview

Thesis research focusing on adaptive reuse of Pittsburgh office buildings from the 1965-1985 era, analyzing their potential for residential conversion with emphasis on daylighting performance and spatial quality.

Research Focus

  • Multiple parametric building iterations using RhinoPython
  • Comprehensive daylighting analysis using Climate Studio
  • Focus on 1965-1985 office building typologies in Pittsburgh
  • Evaluation of spatial daylight autonomy (sDA) metrics

Methodology

  • Parametric model generation for exploring design alternatives
  • Daylighting performance simulation and optimization
  • Building systems analysis for residential conversion feasibility
  • Integration of computational design with building performance metrics
  • Automated workflows for rapid iteration and analysis

Key Challenges

  • Deep floor plates limiting natural light penetration
  • Structural grid constraints on unit layouts
  • Building systems adaptation for residential requirements
  • Balancing unit count with daylighting quality

Tools & Technologies

Rhino, Grasshopper, RhinoPython, Climate Studio, Spatial Daylight Autonomy Analysis

Shaping Daylight Workshop

Overview

Intensive workshop focused on understanding and optimizing daylight performance in buildings. Explored the relationship between spatial quality, occupant comfort, and quantitative daylight metrics through parametric iteration and simulation-based analysis.

Key Metrics Analyzed

  • Spatial Daylight Autonomy (sDA): Percentage of space meeting daylight thresholds
  • Annual Sunlight Exposure (ASE): Hours of direct sunlight penetration
  • Daylight Glare Probability (DGP): Occupant visual comfort assessment
  • View Quality: Quantitative analysis of outside visibility

Design Exploration

  • Parametric shading device design for optimal solar control
  • Window-to-wall ratio optimization studies
  • Glazing specification analysis (VLT, SHGC trade-offs)
  • Orientation studies for various climates

Tools

Climate Studio, Rhino, Grasshopper, Radiance, Annual Daylight Metrics (sDA, ASE, DGP)

Environmental Justice Analysis: Louisiana Cancer Alley

Overview

Comprehensive GIS analysis examining spatial relationships between industrial facilities and community demographics in Louisiana's "Cancer Alley" region, investigating environmental justice implications through multi-layered spatial analysis.

Research Questions

  • What is the spatial distribution of industrial facilities along the Mississippi River?
  • How do facility concentrations correlate with community demographics?
  • Which communities are most exposed to industrial proximity?

Methodology

  • Kernel Density Analysis: Identified industrial facility clustering patterns
  • Buffer Zone Analysis: Calculated population within proximity thresholds
  • Demographic Correlation: Analyzed race, income, and health data relationships
  • Temporal Analysis: Tracked facility growth over decades

Technical Approach

  • ModelBuilder for automated, reproducible analysis workflows
  • Python scripting for batch processing and data management
  • Spatial statistics for correlation analysis
  • Professional cartographic design for clear communication

Tools

ArcGIS Pro, ModelBuilder, Python (ArcPy), Spatial Statistics, Kernel Density Analysis, Cartographic Design
